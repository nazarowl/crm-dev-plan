Roadmap

CRM Milestones

These milestones reflect the revised roadmap: start from the real CRM already running on the VPS, bring that product under repository control, then align and extend it toward the final Aqua Plus target.

Milestone Sequence

Milestone 1

Production Baseline Captured

The VPS CRM is treated as the real baseline and its modules, data structures, storage dependencies, and auth behavior are documented.

Definition of done
  • Current VPS modules are inventoried and confirmed.
  • Major tables, policies, RPCs, and storage buckets are mapped.
  • Migration assumptions are documented.
Milestone 2

Repository Owns the Current CRM

The real CRM baseline is moved into this repository or faithfully reproduced from it without material regression.

Included modules
  • Authentication, roles, permissions, dashboard, leads, activities, pipeline
  • Contacts view, calendar, quotes, templates, settings, and admin
Milestone 3

Aqua Plus Alignment

The CRM reflects Aqua Plus terminology, service taxonomy, stage model, staff profiles, and lead source handling.

Milestone 4

Customer Dossier

The product moves beyond isolated module records into one coherent client history spanning the operational lifecycle.

Milestone 5

Intake and Automation Layer

Website-originated work enters the CRM automatically and triggers confirmations, internal alerts, and follow-up support behavior.

Milestone 6

Booking Integration

Public booking is connected to seller availability, internal scheduling, and customer records.

Milestone 7

Billing Operations

Invoices, quote-to-invoice conversion, payment obligations, payment tracking, and financial history become part of the CRM.

Milestone 8

Cutover Readiness

The migrated and extended CRM is release-ready from this repository with verification, migration validation, and rollback planning.

Recommended Release Order

1. Production baseline captured
2. Repository owns the current CRM
3. Aqua Plus alignment
4. Customer dossier
5. Intake and automation layer
6. Booking integration
7. Billing operations
8. Cutover readiness
